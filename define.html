Misinformation

misinfərˈmāSH(ə)n

false or inaccurate information, especially that which is deliberately intended to deceive.

(Oxford Languages)

Synnonyms

  • Falsity
  • Misreport
  • Inacuracy
  • Distortion
(Thesaurus)

Reboot is a Foundation headed by Helen Lee Bouygues, focused on advancing critial thinking skills. Reboots report "Fighting Fake News" found that reading articles that teach people how to spot misinformation and fake stories can significantly increase the likelyhood of someone spotting one.
(Reboot)

Most misinformation on the web is trivial, and won't cause significant issues in any one person's day to day life, but some stories can have severe consiquenses, such as the ivermectin hoax which caused many Americans to forgo proper treatment for Covid.
